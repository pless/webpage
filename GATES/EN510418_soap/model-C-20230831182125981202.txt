In rural health clinics, malaria diagnostics, such as rapid diagnostic tests (RDTs), are usually available, and blood tests can be used to confirm the diagnosis and differentiate from other conditions like typhoid or typhus. However, molecular diagnostic methods like polymerase chain reaction (PCR) or loop-mediated isothermal amplification (LAMP) can also be used in more complex situations. Finally, while paracetamol is usually advised to relieve fever and headache, its use should be carefully monitored due to the risk of overuse and increased risk of cardiovascular complications.
