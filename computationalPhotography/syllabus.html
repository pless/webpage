CSCI 3907/6907 Computational Photography

Course Description:
This course explores core concepts in design and analysis of systems that co-design the physical process of capturing an image with computational processing required to optimize or reconstruct that image. This merges research and ideas from the areas of computer graphics, computer vision, optics, and imaging, and offers ways to overcome the limitations of traditional cameras. Weekly topics, readings and assignments will be available on the course webpage.
Textbook:

Prerequisites:
This course expect strong programming skills and a course in algorithms (equivalent to either CSCI 3212 or 6212).
Learning outcomes:
At the end of this course, students should be able to (1) describe the main components of the digital image capture pipeline, including the key differences between different types of digital image sensors (2) develop proficiency in various image processing techniques, including filtering, frequency domain analysis and transformations, image pyramids, etc., (3) write programs in Matlab or python or similar to implement algorithms for texture synthesis and hole filling; intelligent scissors; compositing, blending, matting and mosaicing; warping and morphing, and (4) use basic computer vision techniques to synthesize images, including image alignment; multi-perspective panoramas; image-based re-lighting and rendering, etc.
